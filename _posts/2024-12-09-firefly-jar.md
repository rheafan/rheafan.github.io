title: "firefly jar"
---

“under and over” you whisper,

pushing our fingers through silk as you tell me what your mother told you and what her mother told her;

that we are spit from the belly of a pale coil in faint sentience,

that they dragged impossible swathes of moonlight from our mouths till it 

unraveled 

in a heap at the feet of our cruel-eyed conqueror.
<br><br><br>
her red red mouth is

smiling at me so wide i think the rest of her will vanish

—like that cat from the storybooks, or was it a lion?

Love, she murmured,

is beautiful, even

if you are bleeding out onto linoleum in yesterday’s clothes, and no one can hear your swallowed cries.

maybe that word will chafe our lips like it did our mothers,

or perhaps our palates are more accustomed to bitterness than we know.

we lie together and stare at a starry plaster ceiling

like a pair of leafless stalks stretching toward the sun.
<br><br><br>
she hoards heartbreaks like fireflies in jars, and

i watch as their abdomens wink out, one by one like the city after christmas.

each time she says it is the last;

but unlike me she is brave enough to be selfish.

and so she stands —

hands clasped and eyes clenched shut —

for every eyelash blown to some nondescript place, 

every star scraping its fingernails across the night sky,

in the end,

i am the one who will wait with her

for the circle to straighten out.
