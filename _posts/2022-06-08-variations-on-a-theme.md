title: "Variations on a Theme"
---

The familiar feeling

of fingers skating over ivory,

skimming the dust lying in between.
<br><br><br>
The familiar pattern

of a set of notes,

practiced over and over till the ink on the pages begins to fade.
<br><br><br>
The familiar moment

of something once in a while,

the tiny wonder, the smallest triumph, the single key picked from a ring of thousands sliding into

the lock -

turning, turning…

tauntingly close to perfection.
<br><br><br>
The familiar panic

of the clock counting down,

tick tock tick tock

the days flying away -

“time waits for no one”, you hear them say -

and then comes the day.
<br><br><br>
The day.

&emsp;&emsp;Seconds 

&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;minutes 

&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;&emsp;hours 

go by, 

too slow and too fast.

Before she steps into the light she

smooths the folds of her dress

takes a deep breath

puts on a brave smile

– the last plates of armor.
<br><br><br>
It all happens at once.

Time seemed to fly away before,

but now it soars out of reach

toward the bright lights.

Your fingers move on their own, your mind frozen

in a sea of 

useless thoughts.
<br><br><br>
Silence

when you finish

is terrifying at first

but then it is the most wonderful thing in the world, 

to be blinded by the lights

to hear nothing but blood rushing in your ears

heart pounding

to the rhythm of applause

but all you can see

is bright.
<br><br><br>
Going home feels a little different.

Still cocooned,

not quite a butterfly yet.

But a skin has been shed nonetheless.

All to be reminded,

yet again

that there is always room for improvement,

that perfection is unattainable.

That good can always be better.

The real work begins again.
